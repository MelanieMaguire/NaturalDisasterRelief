Tips and Prevention Techniques for Natural Disasters


How to Stay Safe During a Natural Disaster
  • Stay informed
    • Listen to local news channels and radio stations for information about safety tips, weather updates, and evacuation procedures
  • Have a plan for evacuation
    • Where you are going to go
    • How will you get there
  • Keep emergency kits on hand
    • Always have flashlights, first aid supplies, batteries, and important identification information
  • Avoid unnecessary risks
    • Do not leave your home
  • Go to the safest area in your home
    • If there is a flood, go to a higher floor
    • If there is a tornado, go to the basement or inner room on the bottom floor of your home


Safety Tips for Hail Storms
  • Try not to bring in possessions once hail starts to fall to avoid injuries
  • Park cars in garages or covered areas
  • Make sure to bring furniture and other valuables inside
    • Such as outdoor furniture, grills, toys, and other recreational equipment
  • Keep clear of windows and draw curtains to protect from shattered glass
  • Assess damage immediately after storm has passed
    • Such as roof damage, metal gutters, and damage to windows and doors


Safety Tips During a Tornado or High Winds
  • Know when tornadoes occur
    • Tornadoes can occur any time of the year
  • Know what to look for
    • Skies that turn dark and often green
    • A wall cloud
    • Severe thunderstorms with lightning, heavy rain, and sometimes hail
    • A roaring sound
  • Secure large appliances with cables or metal straps
  • Add weight to garage doors or replace them if they are not in good conditions
  • Secure siding
  • Arrange items in your house and make sure that heavy items are on or close to the floor
  • Get under something sturdy, such as a heavy table or workbench
  • Use covers to protect windows and doors
  • Repair any roofing that may be loose or damaged
  • Remove or trim trees or other landscaping that might cause damage in high winds
  • Have an evacuation plan
  • If there is no shelter nearby, lie flat in the nearest ditch


Home Safety Tips During Hurricanes
  • Remove or trim trees and bushes
  • Add weight to garage doors or replace them to prevent wind damage
  • Secure and reinforce windows and doors
  • Check and repair any loose roofing, gutters, or sprouts
  • Take valuables inside
  • Prepare a disaster supply kit
    • Including items such as blankets, flashlights, a first aid kit, a generator, and extra food and water


How to Stay Safe During a Flood
  • Store valuables in a watertight safe
  • Back up important documents online or in a safe, waterproof place
  • Prepare an evacuation plan
  • Stock emergency and first aid supplies
  • Keep extra batteries and flashlights nearby
  • Go to the highest floor of a building


Safety Tips to Keep in Mind During an Earthquake
  • Know when they occur
    • They cannot be predicted
  • What to look for
    • Foreshock
      • An earthquake that occurs before a main shock of an earthquake
    • Aftershock
      • A smaller earthquake that often follows the main shock of a large earthquake
  • Discuss an evacuation plan
  • Plan a second way to exit each room or area
  • Make sure to know where your emergency food and supplies are located
  • Keep several flashlights nearby and make sure they are easily accessible
  • Obtain a NOAA Weather Radio with the public alert feature so you can be notified of any weather updates
  • Locate safe spaces away from windows in each room
  • Prepare a disaster kit
    • Include items such as emergency food, water, batteries, flashlights, medication, and other necessary supplies
